The block statement encapsulates the task(s) that you want to group together.
The rescue statement specifies the task(s) to be executed if any task within the block fails.
The always statement specifies the task(s) that will always be executed regardless of the success or failure of the tasks within the block.
The notify statement triggers the associated handler(s) when the tasks within the block complete.
The handler defines the task(s) that will be executed when triggered by the notify statement.
